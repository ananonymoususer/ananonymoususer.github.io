Moment of Silence for Afghan Civilians Killed in War, One of Many Changes to Forces Appreciation Night

Thursday, 01.30.2014 / 7:30 PM / News
Toronto Maple Leafs
Comment
Print

The Toronto Maple Leafs, always at the centre of the hockey universe, are leading the way once more, adopting a new model for honouring the sacrifice of the troops that they hope other professional sports franchises will adopt.

At Forces Appreciation Night this Saturday, February 1st 2014, for the first time at a professional sporting event, both Canadian soldiers and the innocent civilians killed in war will be honoured.

The names of a selection of the thousands of Afghan victims will be read aloud during a moment of silence on Saturday night and a prayer delivered, to commemorate those innocent lives lost and to demonstrate the Maple Leafs' respect for the sanctity of all life.

For Lupe's Troops, an event for every home game in which a Canadian soldier receives free tickets and is honoured midway through the first period, free tickets will now also be available for Afghan-Canadians who have lost loved ones in Afghanistan.

Maple Leafs Sports and Entertainment (MLSE) believes that given the racial and religious diversity of our organization, and our fanbase, this will be a unique and innovative way to reach out to the community and make hockey a game for everyone. We do not see a difference in the value of the life of a Canadian and the life of an Afghan, and we look forward to the day when Canadians and Afghans alike can enjoy sports and entertainment in peace.

Saturday will also mark the beginning of a new Maple Leafs initiative to support veterans of the Afghan war, by raising awareness of the void left by the federal government in supporting veterans with disabilities, mental health issues, and injuries rendering them unable to work and forcing them into poverty.

The troops are the ones fighting the government's wars abroad, and the fact that the government has turned their backs on the thousands of veterans after they've come home means that MLSE needs to step up and do our part.

MLSE is glad to join the 80% of Canadians who wish to support the troops by ending the war that has caused such suffering for both Canadians and Afghans.

Authors: Tommie Smith and John Carlos
